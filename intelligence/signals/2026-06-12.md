- **[Snowflake]** **NEW VENDOR (not previously tracked).** At **Snowflake Summit 2026 (June 1–4, San Francisco)** Snowflake announced **Agent Identity** — every Snowflake-native AI agent (Cortex, CoCo) gets a **cryptographically verified identity** that must be established *before* the agent can access data or act. Access controls are **per-agent, not inherited from the launching user's credentials**, enabling per-agent dynamic data masking, row-access policies, and full audit trails. Folds into the Horizon Catalog governance layer. GA for first-party agents; third-party agent coverage in progress. This pushes first-class agent identity down into the **data layer** — a different locus than the IAM-vendor agent-ID products already logged. — [Flexera Summit recap](https://www.flexera.com/blog/perspectives/snowflake-summit-2026/) / [Snowflake Horizon Catalog press release](https://www.snowflake.com/en/news/press-releases/snowflake-advances-trusted-ai-with-snowflake-horizon-catalog-centralizing-governance-context-and-security-across-the-enterprise/) — capability: C1, C3, C9 — domain: IAM-for-AI

## Notable
(Lower-priority but worth logging.)

- **[Snowflake / Natoma]** **BACKFILL (May 27, 2026; supports the Summit GA above).** Snowflake announced intent to **acquire Natoma**, an enterprise **MCP platform** for AI agents. The deal is positioned to give Snowflake a **natively integrated identity + governance layer for AI agents and MCP tool access** — Natoma enforces identity verification, access policies, and audit controls at the level of **individual tool calls** (who requested an action, what permissions they hold, whether to allow it). Signals Snowflake's intent to extend Agent Identity to third-party/cross-environment agents via MCP. — [Snowflake press release](https://www.snowflake.com/en/news/press-releases/snowflake-announces-intent-to-acquire-natoma-providing-secure-connectivity-for-the-agentic-enterprise/) / [CIO coverage](https://www.cio.com/article/4178160/snowflake-to-acquire-mcp-focused-natoma-to-boost-governance-for-ai-agents.html) — capability: C1, C3, C4 — domain: IAM-for-AI

- **[SailPoint]** **NEW VENDOR / BACKFILL (May 11, 2026).** SailPoint launched **Agentic Fabric**, extending IGA-style identity governance to autonomous AI agents: **discovery** (inventory of agents, machine identities, apps across cloud), **governance** (map every agent to a human owner + lifecycle/access policy), and **protection** (real-time authorization controls + threat detection). Two packages — *Agentic Business* (least-privilege) and *Agentic Business Plus* (zero-standing-privilege, JIT access) — both targeting **GA summer 2026**. — [SailPoint press release](https://investor.sailpoint.com/news-releases/news-release-details/sailpoint-launches-agentic-fabric-secure-ai-identities-across) / [Help Net Security](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/05/11/sailpoint-agentic-fabric-expands-identity-governance-to-autonomous-ai-agents/) — capability: C1, C3, C9 — domain: IAM-for-AI

- **[Curity]** **NEW VENDOR / BACKFILL (~April 15, 2026).** Curity launched **Access Intelligence** — runtime authorization for enterprise AI agents inside the Curity Identity Server. Treats agents as a special application class credentialed via OAuth, and adds **Token Intelligence**: OAuth tokens carry the agent's **purpose/intent**, and access is granted only for that declared purpose, re-evaluated **per step** rather than at a one-time authN event. — [Curity news](https://curity.io/news/curity-announces-access-intelligence/) / [CSO Online](https://www.csoonline.com/article/4158847/curity-looks-to-reinvent-iam-with-runtime-authorization-for-ai-agents.html) — capability: C3, C7 — domain: IAM-for-AI

- **[Red Hat / SPIFFE]** Red Hat Emerging Tech published **"Wiring zero trust identity for AI agents: SPIFFE, token exchange, and Kagenti"** (**June 10, 2026**) — a reference architecture binding real SPIFFE identities to agents via an Envoy-based **AuthBridge sidecar** doing **RFC 8693 token exchange**, alongside Keycloak. Explainer/architecture, not a product release, but signals momentum on the SPIFFE-for-agents pattern.
